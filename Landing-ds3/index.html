Overview

Personalizing the Impersonal is a data visualization project that explores the spatial, temporal, and social dimensions of the Providence Athenaeum, one of America's oldest independent libraries.

Through mapping circulation patterns, collection taxonomies, and architectural spaces, this project reveals the invisible networks that connect readers, books, and knowledge across 190 years of cultural memory.

Methodology

We analyzed over 200,000 catalogued items, 52,000+ circulation events, and conducted spatial surveys of the building's architecture to create a comprehensive data portrait of the library.

The visualizations combine quantitative data analysis with qualitative spatial observation to reveal patterns in how knowledge circulates through physical and temporal space.
